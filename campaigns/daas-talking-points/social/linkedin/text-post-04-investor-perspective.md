Five investors. One message. Data wins.

Bessemer Venture Partners: "Defensibility stems from domain expertise: integrations, data moats, and multimodal interfaces built for vertical-specific needs."

Harsha Kapre, Snowflake Ventures: "The winners will be those who combine incumbent domain expertise with AI-native technical leadership."

Ryan Hinkle, Insight Partners: "SaaS applications that are systems of work will be huge beneficiaries of AI. Filing cabinets are threatened."

Fergus Jarvis, BCG: "Companies without a clearly articulated AI strategy are getting a 30%+ haircut."

Sandeep Kondury: "2026 belongs to teams who treat data as infrastructure and AI as a decision engine."

The pattern is clear.

Software that stores information is getting commoditized. Software powered by proprietary data that drives action is getting more valuable.

Systems of work beat filing cabinets. Proprietary data beats generic. Domain expertise beats general-purpose.

If you're building in B2B data, the investor thesis has never been more aligned with what you're doing.
