The market has flipped, and most people haven't caught up yet.

Data Infrastructure companies now trade at 24.4x EBITDA.

Median SaaS? 12.7x.

Analytics and Data Management was the only software category to expand in valuation multiples last year. Every other category contracted.

In February, $285 billion in SaaS market cap was erased in a single trading day. Traders called it the SaaSpocalypse.

Meanwhile, companies combining AI with proprietary data moats are trading at 30-50x revenue. Traditional SaaS is at 4-6x.

Erik Matlick at Bombora put it simply: "AI replicates software. It cannot replicate unique data. Data is AI's input layer. Software is the output layer being commoditized."

The hottest trade on the Street right now has a name: HALO. Hard Assets, Low Obsolescence.

If your company owns proprietary data, the math has never been better.

If it doesn't, the math has never been worse.
