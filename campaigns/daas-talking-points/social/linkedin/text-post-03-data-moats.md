"Long-term defensibility is more likely to come from differentiated technology and domain expertise, with data a key fuel for both."

That's from a16z — the most prominent voice arguing that data moats don't exist.

Read it again.

Even the biggest data moat skeptic says defensibility comes from differentiated technology + domain expertise + data as fuel.

That's three things. Most companies have one. Maybe two.

If you have all three — proprietary technology that collects unique data, deep domain expertise built over years, and a compounding data asset — you have a moat that gets deeper over time, not shallower.

Warren Buffett: "A truly great business must have an enduring moat that protects excellent returns on invested capital."

Peter Thiel: "Every monopoly is unique, but they usually share some combination of proprietary technology, network effects, economies of scale, and branding."

The frameworks are consistent. The question is whether your data is generic and replicable, or proprietary and compounding.

One is a commodity. The other is a moat.
