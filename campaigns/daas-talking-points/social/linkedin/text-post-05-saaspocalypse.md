What the SaaSpocalypse actually means for B2B data companies.

February 2026: $285 billion in SaaS market cap erased in one trading day.

SaaS stocks down 20%+ since late 2025. One of the fastest drawdowns outside of 2008 and the 2022 tech unwind.

But here's what's easy to miss in the headline:

Data Infrastructure EBITDA multiple: 24.4x.
Median SaaS EBITDA multiple: 12.7x.

AI + data moat companies: 30-50x revenue.
Traditional SaaS: 4-6x revenue.

Erik Matlick at Bombora: "What were perceived as SaaS strengths have become weaknesses. What were perceived as DaaS weaknesses have become strengths."

DaaS never carried inflated valuations built on hypergrowth expectations. The model was always steady, recurring, and compounding. Turns out that's exactly what the market wants right now.

Luke Lango at InvestorPlace calls it the HALO trade: Hard Assets, Low Obsolescence. Companies with physical infrastructure or proprietary data are the only ones soaring while pure-play software gets demolished.

The boring data company was never boring. The market is just catching up.
