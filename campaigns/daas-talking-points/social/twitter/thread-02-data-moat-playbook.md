# Thread: The Data Moat Playbook

**Tweet 1 (Hook):**
"Data moats don't exist" is the most expensive belief in B2B right now.

Here's what the smartest investors actually say about data defensibility:

**Tweet 2:**
Bessemer Venture Partners:

"Defensibility stems from domain expertise: integrations, data moats, and multimodal interfaces built for vertical-specific needs."

Domain expertise + data + integrations. You need all three.

**Tweet 3:**
Harsha Kapre (Snowflake Ventures):

"The winners will be those who combine incumbent domain expertise with AI-native technical leadership."

Years of domain expertise can't be replicated in a sprint. That's the moat.

**Tweet 4:**
Warren Buffett: "Economic castles protected by unbreachable moats."

Peter Thiel: "Proprietary technology, network effects, economies of scale, and branding."

The frameworks are decades old. Data just became the strongest version of them.

**Tweet 5:**
Attainment Labs:

"Proprietary data compounds over time. The longer the product operates, the deeper the moat becomes."

Software depreciates. Data appreciates. That's the whole game.

**Tweet 6:**
The a16z objection: "Long-term defensibility comes from differentiated technology and domain expertise, with data a key fuel for both."

Even the skeptics agree data is fuel. The question is whether yours is generic or proprietary.

Generic data = commodity.
Proprietary, first-party, compounding data = moat.

**Tweet 7:**
Bain & Company:

"Your data is your moat. While models such as GPT-4o are everywhere, the real value lies in the proprietary data you own."

If your AI strategy depends on data anyone can buy, you don't have a strategy. You have a feature.
