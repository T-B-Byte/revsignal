# Thread: The DaaS vs SaaS Reckoning

**Tweet 1 (Hook):**
$285 billion in SaaS market cap erased in a single day.

Meanwhile, data companies are trading at nearly 2x the SaaS multiple.

The market is telling you something. Here's what:

**Tweet 2:**
Data Infrastructure: 24.4x EBITDA
Median SaaS: 12.7x EBITDA

Analytics & Data Management was the ONLY software category to expand in multiples last year. Everything else contracted.

**Tweet 3:**
Erik Matlick (CEO, Bombora):

"AI replicates software. It cannot replicate unique data. Data is AI's input layer. Software is the output layer being commoditized."

This is the whole thesis in four sentences.

**Tweet 4:**
Ali Ghodsi (CEO, Databricks):

"It's a commodity now to have AI that has general knowledge. It's very elusive to get AI that really works and understands that proprietary data that's inside enterprise."

**Tweet 5:**
The math:

- AI + proprietary data moat = 30-50x revenue
- Traditional SaaS = 4-6x revenue
- No AI strategy = 30%+ valuation haircut (BCG)

The spread has never been wider.

**Tweet 6:**
Wall Street has a name for this: the HALO trade.

Hard Assets, Low Obsolescence.

Companies with physical infrastructure or proprietary data. The only things AI cannot generate.

**Tweet 7:**
The counter-argument from a16z: "Data moats are an empty promise."

But read their actual quote: defensibility comes from "differentiated technology and domain expertise, with data a key fuel for both."

They're arguing against generic data. Not proprietary, first-party, compounding data assets.

**Tweet 8:**
Bottom line:

AI commoditizes software. It increases demand for data.

If you own proprietary data, the market is rewarding you.

If you don't, the market is punishing you.

The SaaSpocalypse isn't a correction. It's a repricing.
