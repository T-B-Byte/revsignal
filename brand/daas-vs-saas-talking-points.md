# DaaS vs SaaS: Power Quotes & Talking Points

> Compiled March 2026. Use these in sales conversations, pitch decks, and marketing content.

---

## The Core Argument (Erik Matlick, CEO, Bombora)

"AI replicates software. It cannot replicate unique data. Data is AI's input layer. Software is the output layer being commoditized."

"DaaS never carried inflated valuations built on hypergrowth expectations. Slow and steady turns out to be a feature, not a bug."

"What were perceived as SaaS strengths have become weaknesses. What were perceived as DaaS weaknesses have become strengths."

"AI agents interpret data faster than humans ever could. More AI systems = more demand for proprietary data."

Source: "Will 'Boring' DaaS Escape the AI-Fueled Slaughter of SaaS?" (LinkedIn, Feb 2026)

## Data is the Moat

"The real moat is no longer the model, but who controls clean, fast, compliant data pipelines."
— Sandeep Kondury, Angel Investor

"The moat is not the software itself but what the software has ingested over time."
— Attainment Labs

"Proprietary data compounds over time. The longer the product operates, the deeper the moat becomes."
— Attainment Labs

"If you don't own that data, you compete on price. If you do, you compete on relevance."
— Velou Commerce Analysis

"2026 belongs to teams who treat data as infrastructure and AI as a decision engine."
— Sandeep Kondury, Angel Investor

"Your data is your moat. While models such as GPT-4o are everywhere, the real value lies in the proprietary data you own."
— Bain & Company

## AI Commoditizes Software, Not Data

"It's a commodity now to have AI that has general knowledge. It's very elusive to get AI that really works and understands that proprietary data that's inside enterprise."
— Ali Ghodsi, CEO, Databricks

"The next wave of AI success is going to be completely dependent on structured data."
— Ali Ghodsi, CEO, Databricks

"Most 'AI software' will get vaporized as models get cheaper/better."
— Luke Lango, Senior Investment Analyst, InvestorPlace

"These companies possess the only two things AI cannot generate: real-world physical infrastructure or proprietary, unstructured, regulated data."
— Luke Lango, InvestorPlace

"AI is commoditizing 'cognition,' and the only thing that matters now is who can operationalize it inside real organizations, fast, securely, and at scale."
— Luke Lango, InvestorPlace

## Classic Moat Frameworks

"In business, I look for economic castles protected by unbreachable moats."
— Warren Buffett, Chairman, Berkshire Hathaway

"A truly great business must have an enduring 'moat' that protects excellent returns on invested capital."
— Warren Buffett

"Every monopoly is unique, but they usually share some combination of: proprietary technology, network effects, economies of scale, and branding."
— Peter Thiel, Co-founder PayPal/Palantir

"The shift to data-centric AI is as important as the shift to deep learning in the past decade."
— Andrew Ng, Co-founder Google Brain

## Investor Perspectives

"Investors are adjusting for the significant premium applied to information services assets over the past few years. Companies without a clearly articulated AI strategy are getting a 30%+ haircut."
— Fergus Jarvis, BCG

"Defensibility stems from domain expertise: integrations, data moats, and multimodal interfaces built for vertical-specific needs."
— Bessemer Venture Partners, State of AI 2025

"SaaS applications that are systems of work will be huge beneficiaries of AI. Filing cabinets are threatened."
— Ryan Hinkle, Managing Director, Insight Partners

"There's a real data-layer bottleneck in enterprise AI deployments that needs addressing first."
— Teddie Wardi, Managing Director, Insight Partners

"The winners will be those who combine incumbent domain expertise with AI-native technical leadership."
— Harsha Kapre, Head of Snowflake Ventures

---

## The Counter-Argument (Know This for Objection Handling)

"Long-term defensibility is more likely to come from differentiated technology and domain expertise, with data a key fuel for both."
— a16z, "The Empty Promise of Data Moats"

**Rebuttal:** a16z is talking about generic data that can be scraped or bought. pharosIQ's data is first-party, permission-based, human-verified, collected over years, with 270M+ contacts and 650+ proprietary intent categories. That is the opposite of generic, replicable data.
