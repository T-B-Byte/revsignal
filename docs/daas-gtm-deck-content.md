## SLIDE 5: Four Deployment Methods

Every DaaS product can be delivered through one or more of these methods. The customer chooses what fits their infrastructure. We support all four.

### 1. API (Real-Time Integration)
Customer integrates pharosIQ data directly into their tech stack via REST API. Data flows in real time, programmatically, on demand.

**Examples:**
- HubSpot pulls intent signals via API to score leads in real time as they enter the CRM
- Clay queries pharosIQ's API as one step in a multi-vendor enrichment waterfall
- A customer's internal data warehouse pulls nightly intent snapshots via scheduled API calls
- Gong calls the API mid-conversation to surface what a prospect was researching before the call

**Best for:** Tech-forward buyers, platform integrations, real-time use cases, customers who want to build workflows around the data.

### 2. Flat File (Bulk Data Delivery)
Scheduled data drops in CSV, JSON, or Parquet format. Delivered via SFTP, cloud storage (S3, Azure Blob), or email.

**Examples:**
- Weekly intent signal exports sent to a customer's S3 bucket every Monday morning
- Monthly contact enrichment files delivered via SFTP for CRM bulk import
- pharosIQ's weekly Surge Trending reports delivered as CSV to customers without API infrastructure
- A PE firm receives quarterly portfolio-wide intent data as Excel files for their analysts

**Best for:** Customers without engineering resources, legacy systems, bulk analytics, teams that process data in spreadsheets or BI tools.

### 3. OEM / Embedded (White-Labeled Into Their Product)
pharosIQ's data is embedded inside another company's product. The end user sees the partner's brand, not pharosIQ's. The partner licenses our data to power their features.

**Examples:**
- HubSpot embeds pharosIQ intent signals into their lead scoring engine. HubSpot users see "intent score" but never see "pharosIQ"
- Apollo adds a "Surging Now" badge to contact records, powered by pharosIQ signals behind the scenes
- Salesforce AppExchange partner builds an "Intent Insights" widget using pharosIQ data, sold under their own brand
- A recruiting platform uses pharosIQ hiring-intent signals to predict which companies are about to start hiring, branded as their own feature

**Best for:** Platform companies that want to enhance their product without building a content network. Highest ACV deals, longest sales cycles, stickiest revenue.

### 4. Cloud / SaaS (Hosted Platform Access)
Customer logs into a pharosIQ-hosted platform to access data, run queries, generate reports, and manage their account through a browser interface. No API integration, no file downloads, no technical setup.

**Examples:**
- **SurgeEngine.ai** — Customer logs in, selects intent topics, sees which companies are surging, generates Surge Dossiers on demand
- ZoomInfo's platform — Users search contacts and companies through ZoomInfo's web app, export lists, set up alerts
- Bombora's Surge Analytics dashboard — Customers log in to see account-level surge activity, filter by geography and industry, export segments
- LinkedIn Sales Navigator — Customers access data through LinkedIn's hosted interface, build lead lists, get alerts on saved accounts
- 6sense's Revenue AI platform — Customers access intent insights, account scoring, and buying stage predictions through 6sense's web app

**Best for:** Teams that want packaged intelligence without technical integration. Fastest time-to-value, lowest onboarding friction. Also the highest-margin model because you control the experience and the customer builds workflows around your UI.

### Key Distinction: Cloud/SaaS vs. OEM
- **Cloud/SaaS:** Customer comes to **our** platform. They see pharosIQ's brand, our UI, our experience. We own the relationship.
- **OEM:** Our data lives inside **their** platform. Their users may never know pharosIQ exists. The partner is our customer, not the end user.

ZoomInfo does both: they have their own platform (Cloud/SaaS) AND they embed their data into Salesforce and HubSpot (OEM). pharosIQ can do the same with SurgeEngine.ai as the Cloud/SaaS play and OEM licensing as a separate revenue stream.

### Why This Matters
Offering all four deployment methods removes the most common objection in data licensing: "How does it fit into our stack?" The answer is always yes, regardless of the customer's technical maturity.

**Account Management Modules:**
- **Account health scorecard** (renewal risk, expansion opportunity)

## SLIDE 10B: PRONG 6 — ICP Analyzer (Closed-Won Intelligence)

### What We Sell
A data product that answers: "What did my best customers have in common before they bought, and who else looks like that right now?"

Customers upload their closed-won deal list. The system cross-references each company against pharosIQ's intent data to find the signals those companies were showing in the weeks and months before they became customers. It synthesizes an "ideal customer profile" based on actual buying behavior (not guesswork), then identifies lookalike companies currently exhibiting the same signal patterns.

### How It Works
1. Customer uploads a list of closed-won accounts (company name, deal size, close date)
2. pharosIQ's intent data reveals what each company was researching before they closed
3. AI identifies common patterns: which signals appear most frequently, how far in advance they appeared, and how strong the correlation is
4. System outputs a data-backed ICP profile with specific buying triggers
5. System identifies lookalike companies currently matching those patterns, ranked by match score
6. Each lookalike includes estimated deal size, recommended entry point, and current surge topics

### Why This Is Different
Every company has an ICP. Most are built from gut feel, firmographic filters, and sales team opinions. This builds an ICP from actual intent signal patterns that preceded closed deals. It turns "companies like our best customers" from a heuristic into a data product.

### Pricing Model
- Per-report: $500 (includes top 25 lookalikes)
- Quarterly refresh: $1,500/quarter
- Annual subscription with monthly lookalike updates: $5,000/year

### Revenue Projection
- Year 1: 400-1,000 reports = **$200K-500K**
- Year 2: Subscription conversion at scale = **$500K-1.0M**

### Target Buyers
- Sales leaders who want data-backed target lists, not opinion-based ones
- RevOps teams building territory plans and account scoring models
