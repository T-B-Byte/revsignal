## The Two Gaps

**Gap 1: Title Fragmentation (Jobson solves this)**
LinkedIn recognizes 55% of job titles. That means 45% of your target audience is invisible to title-based targeting. You're paying full price to reach roughly half your buyers.

**Gap 2: Account-Level Ceiling (pharosIQ Contacts + Intent solves this)**
Most intent data tells you "Company X is surging on cloud security." Great. But who at Company X? Bombora, G2, and TrustRadius give you the account. They can't give you the people. pharosIQ delivers the actual contact records (name, title, verified email) at surging accounts, with persona-level intent signals from first-party content properties (not bid-stream). 650+ intent categories. 360M+ contacts. 1.6M engaged contacts in any 90-day window.

**Combined:** Your campaigns miss half the buyers (title gap) and treat every account equally (intent gap). Fix both and the math changes completely.

## Caveats (Use These to Build Trust)

- The 40% audience lift is from early testing and will vary by title, industry, and platform
- Not all missed impressions convert to leads. The CPL math assumes proportional improvement.
- Intent signal conversion lift (2-3x) is based on industry benchmarks for first-party intent data, not pharosIQ-specific data (yet). Early customer data will refine these numbers.
- Title expansion improves targeting completeness, not targeting relevance. You still need good persona definitions.
- These models assume title-based targeting is a meaningful part of the campaign strategy. For pure account-level campaigns with no title filter, the Jobson impact is smaller (but contacts + intent still applies).
- Tier 3/4 pricing ($144K-$504K) has a different ROI profile than Jobson alone ($50K). The ROI is still positive but the buyer needs higher ad spend or campaign volume to justify it.

**Leading with honest caveats builds credibility.** You're not overselling. You're showing the math and letting the buyer do their own calculation with their own numbers. That's how data products should be sold.
