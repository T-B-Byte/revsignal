## GEOGRAPHY — REGIONAL INTELLIGENCE (90 seconds)

**[Click the Geography tab]**

> "Now let's layer on geography, because where your buyers sit changes everything about how you go to market."

**[Point to the regional distribution]**

> "North America leads with 42.7 million contacts and 118 million signals in 90 days. EMEA is 30.5 million contacts with 93 million signals. APAC is 12 million contacts and 43 million signals. LATAM is 12 million contacts with 9 million signals."

**[STORY: The APAC Signal Density Story]**

> "Now let me show you something that jumps off the page when you look at the signal data.
>
> APAC has 12 million contacts — roughly a quarter the size of North America. But it's generating 43 million signals. That's a signal-to-contact ratio of about 3.6. North America? 118 million signals across 42.7 million contacts — a ratio of about 2.8.
>
> APAC contacts are *more actively researching* per capita than any other region. If you're a product leader debating where to invest your next expansion dollar, that ratio is telling you something. The audience is smaller, but it's *hungrier*. The demand density is higher. You're not going to get that insight from a CRM export or a LinkedIn search. It takes signal data layered on top of audience data to see it."

**[Click the Country Drill-Down tab]**

> "And we go all the way down to the country level. 195 countries, each with seniority breakdowns, role distributions, and industry compositions."

**[Select a major country — e.g., United Kingdom]**

This is how you plan a regional launch — not with TAM estimates from an analyst report, but with *verified contact counts* at the country and role level."

**[STORY: The LATAM Growth Story]**

You can get in front of them *before* they shortlist. ## ENGAGEMENT & COVERAGE — MULTI-CHANNEL PROOF (75 seconds)

**[Click the Engagement & Coverage tab]**

> "The question I always get at this point is: 'Where does this data actually come from?' This tab answers that."

**[Point to the channel breakdown]**

> "We see engagement across five distinct channels:
>
> **Email** — 268,000 companies, 8,300 topics, 351 million signals. This is our deepest signal source. When someone opens, clicks, or engages with email content about a specific topic, we capture that as a signal.
>
> **Review Sites** — 63,600 companies, 528 topics, 189 million signals. This is arguably our *highest-value* signal source."

**[STORY: The Review Site Story]**

> "Think about what it means when someone is on a review site. They're not in awareness mode. They're not reading a thought-leadership blog. They're *comparing vendors*. They're reading pricing pages, feature comparisons, customer reviews. That's bottom-of-funnel intent. When we see a company generating review site signals for 'marketing automation' or 'CRM,' that company is *actively evaluating solutions in your category*. That's not a lead — that's an opportunity."

> "**Display** — 74,000 companies across 33 topics, 2.6 million signals. This captures content consumption through display advertising and content recommendation engines.
>
> **Content Syndication** — 53,000 companies engaging with syndicated content assets. These are people who downloaded a whitepaper, watched a webinar, or consumed gated content.
>
> **Voice** — 291 companies, 550 topics, 2,100 signals. Real human conversations — phone-based engagement."

**[STORY: The Multi-Channel Conviction Story]**

> "Here's why the multi-channel picture matters. Any single channel can give you a false positive. Someone might accidentally click an email. Someone might browse a review site out of curiosity. But when the same company generates signals across *email, review sites, and content syndication* — all for the same topic — that's not noise. That's a buying signal with conviction behind it.
>
> 692,000 companies generated signals in the last year. 360,000 in the last 90 days. We're seeing these companies across multiple surfaces, and that multi-touch view is what makes our intent data *reliable*, not just big."
