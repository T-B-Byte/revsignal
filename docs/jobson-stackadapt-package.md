### Executive Summary

pharosIQ proposes a two-phase data partnership with StackAdapt:

**Phase 1:** Integrate Jobson, our title expansion API, into StackAdapt's B2B audience building workflow. Jobson expands any job title into every real-world variation from 360M+ verified business contacts, increasing audience coverage by approximately 40% without changing the end-user experience.

**Phase 2:** Integrate pharosIQ's full contacts + intent data feed as an OEM data source. StackAdapt customers get verified contact records at surging accounts, with persona-level intent signals and expanded titles, delivered natively inside the platform. This transforms StackAdapt's B2B offering from account-level targeting to person-level targeting.

Both phases are complementary to StackAdapt's existing data partners (Bombora, Leadspace, Lead Forensics, Experian) and fill gaps that none of them address.

---

### The Problem (Two Layers)

**Layer 1: Title Fragmentation**

Job titles are freeform text. The same buyer appears under dozens or hundreds of title variations across LinkedIn, CRMs, and job boards. When a StackAdapt customer filters by "VP of Marketing," the platform can only match against titles it recognizes.

Key data points:
- LinkedIn recognizes only 55% of job titles on its platform
- B2B programmatic match rates range from 25-55%
- The average enterprise has 18,400 unique title strings for what should be 900 roles
- B2B contact data decays at 30%+ per year, with titles changing faster than emails

Every unrecognized title variation is a buyer the campaign never reaches.

**Layer 2: Account-Level Ceiling**

StackAdapt's ecosystem currently tells customers which accounts are surging (Bombora), what those companies look like (Leadspace), and who visited their website (Lead Forensics). But none of these partners deliver the actual verified contact records of the buyers at those accounts, with persona-level intent signals showing what each individual cares about.

The result: customers know which accounts to target, but they're still guessing about which people to reach at those accounts. This limits campaign precision and increases wasted ad spend.

---

### Phase 1: Jobson Title Expansion API

**What it does:** Takes a single job title as input and returns every real-world variation, including:

- Semantic variations (VP of Marketing, Vice President Marketing, VP Marketing & Communications)
- Seniority variations (Head of Marketing, SVP Marketing Strategy, CMO)
- Functional overlaps (VP Demand Gen & Marketing, VP Growth, VP Brand & Marketing)
- International translations (12+ languages)
- Industry-specific variations (VP Marketing, Technology vs. VP Marketing, Financial Services)

**Data source:** 360M+ verified business contact records from pharosIQ's proprietary database. Every variation comes from an actual person's actual profile. This is ground truth, not NLP inference or synonym generation.

**Integration architecture:**

```
1. StackAdapt customer creates B2B audience
2. Customer enters target job title(s) in audience builder
3. StackAdapt calls Jobson API with seed title(s)
4. Jobson returns expanded title variations (200+ per seed title)
5. StackAdapt applies expanded titles to audience matching
6. Customer sees larger, more accurate audience, activates campaign
```

**Technical details:**
- Single REST API endpoint
- Response time: <500ms per title
- JSON response format with title variations, confidence scores, and language tags
- Rate limiting: configurable per StackAdapt's volume needs
- Authentication: API key
- No PII transmitted or stored (titles only, not contacts)

**End-user experience:** Zero workflow change. The expansion happens behind the scenes. Customer types "VP of Marketing" and StackAdapt's audience builder automatically includes all 200+ variations. The customer sees a larger matched audience and better campaign performance.

Optional: surface the expansion to the end user as a "Title Expansion" toggle or "See all title variations" disclosure in the audience builder. This becomes a visible feature differentiator.
