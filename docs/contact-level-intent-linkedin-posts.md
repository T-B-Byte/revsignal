## POST 2: The 11-Person Buying Committee (Stat Bomb)

---

The average B2B buying committee now has 11 to 13 stakeholders.

Read that again. 11 to 13 people influence the purchase decision.

Your ABM platform knows the account is surging. Great. Now what?

Your sales team searches "CISO" and finds 3 contacts. But the committee includes "VP Cybersecurity and Risk," "Head of InfoSec," "Director, Security Architecture," and 150 other title variations.

Your intent signal was perfect. Your title data killed the activation.

72% of marketers say contact-level ABM is a core differentiator for their program. 24% of teams plan to implement it in the next 12 months.

But here's the math nobody talks about:

If your title taxonomy captures 55% of real job titles (LinkedIn's own match rate), and your buying committee has 13 people, you're reaching maybe 7 of them. The other 6 are invisible. Not because they don't exist. Because their titles don't match your targeting list.

That's not a strategy problem. That's a data infrastructure problem.

Account-level intent + persona-level intelligence = the full picture.

## POST 3: The "One Motion" Shift (Practitioner Angle)

---

The smartest B2B teams I'm seeing right now have stopped treating ABM, outbound, and paid as separate campaigns.

One account list. Same buying committee. Fed simultaneously into:

→ Paid ads (LinkedIn, Meta, programmatic)
→ Outbound email sequences
→ LinkedIn outreach
→ Remarketing

Same people. Same message. Every channel.

This only works when you know exactly who the right people are at each account. Not just "Director of IT." Every variation of that title. Every member of the buying committee. Every way to reach them.

The teams doing this well are seeing 74% higher meeting conversion and 2-4x more pipeline than teams running account-level campaigns.

The difference isn't the channels. It's the contact resolution underneath.

If your outbound list and your ad audience aren't the same people, you're running two campaigns. If they are, you're running one motion.

## POST 4: The Infrastructure Problem (Thought Leadership)

---

Everyone is building the activation layer for B2B advertising.

Influ2 delivers ads to named individuals. Clay enriches contacts for Meta targeting. Demandbase maps buying groups with AI. Metadata.io automates campaign execution.

All excellent products. But they all share the same dependency:

The quality of your contact data.

If your database has 150M contacts, you're missing half the market. If your title taxonomy doesn't expand "VP of Marketing" into the 200+ real-world variations, every platform downstream inherits that gap.

Account-level intent without persona resolution is like GPS without a destination. You know something is happening. You don't know which roles to engage or what topics they care about.

The companies building the intelligence layer underneath, the ones with first-party persona + intent topic data, title resolution across languages, and buying committee intelligence that tells you which roles are actively evaluating, those are the ones that will define the next generation of B2B advertising.

Not because they target better. Because they make everyone else's targeting smarter.

## POST 5: The Question Post (Engagement Play)

---

Quick question for anyone running B2B campaigns:

When you build a target audience, how do you handle job title variations?

I've been looking at the data, and here's what surprised me:

→ LinkedIn only recognizes 55% of the titles on its own platform
→ "VP of Marketing" has 200+ real-world variations
→ The average enterprise has 18,400 unique title strings for 900 actual roles
→ B2B data decays 30%+ per year, and titles change faster than emails

Some teams are manually building title lists in spreadsheets. Some have given up on title targeting entirely and use Job Function + Seniority instead (more reach, less precision).

And some are using title expansion engines that map one seed title to every real permutation across millions of real contacts.

What's your approach? Curious how different teams are solving this.
