# MARKET ASSESSMENT PROMPT — METADATA.IO
# Copy everything below this line into a new Claude Code session.

---

I want you to conduct a deep market and competitive assessment for **Metadata.io**, a company in the **B2B demand generation / marketing automation** space. Their product/service is an AI-powered demand generation platform that automates paid campaigns (LinkedIn, Facebook, display) using intent data, audience targeting, and campaign experimentation to help B2B marketers generate pipeline more efficiently. Their website is https://metadata.io.

Run this assessment in phases. Complete each phase fully before moving to the next. Use the /last30days skill for the initial research sweep, then supplement heavily with web searches across financial databases, press releases, analyst reports, Glassdoor, Crunchbase, PitchBook references, and trade publications.

---

## PHASE 1: Company Deep Dive

Research and present:
- What the company does, core products/platforms, and how they make money
- Company history (founding, key milestones, pivots, mergers)
- Leadership team with backgrounds (CEO, C-suite, founders) — Gil Allouche is the founder and still CEO as of Feb 2026. Research his background and the rest of the leadership team. Check for any notable departures or additions in the last 12 months.
- Employee count and geographic footprint — look for trends (growing or shrinking?)
- Recent news and announcements (last 6-12 months)
- Technology and product evolution timeline

## PHASE 2: Ownership & Capital Structure

Research and present:
- Ownership structure (who owns what %, founder vs investor vs public)
- Total funding raised, by round, with investors named — they raised a Series B in 2021 from Resolute Ventures, Greycroft, and others. Has there been anything since? A down round?
- Current valuation (or market cap if public) and how it's changed over time
- Revenue (actual or estimated) and growth rate
- Revenue multiple (current valuation / revenue)
- Debt obligations or credit facilities
- Any PE involvement — who, when, what fund, typical hold period
- Is there a known or likely exit timeline?
- Do employees have equity? Any evidence of ESOP, stock options, phantom equity?

## PHASE 3: Competitive Landscape

Research and present:
- Identify ALL major competitors (direct and adjacent) — include but don't limit to: RollWorks, Terminus, HockeyStack, Demandbase (advertising side), 6sense (advertising side), Influ2, N.Rich, Triblio
- For EACH competitor, build the same profile: revenue, funding, valuation, growth rate, ownership, key differentiators
- Build a comparison table across all competitors covering: revenue, funding raised, current valuation, revenue multiple, growth rate, employee count, key product, and one-line differentiator
- Forrester Wave, Gartner Magic Quadrant, or G2 rankings if they exist for this category (B2B advertising, demand gen, ABM platforms)
- What does Reddit, X, and practitioner sentiment say about each competitor? Who do people actually like vs. complain about?

## PHASE 4: Funding & Valuation Scorecard

Build a comprehensive table of ALL competitors showing:
- Total investor funding raised
- Peak valuation vs current valuation
- Revenue multiple (current value / revenue)
- % change from peak valuation
- Growth trajectory (accelerating, stable, decelerating, declining)
- Zombie assessment: Is each company healthy, wounded, or dying? Support with evidence (layoffs, impairment charges, stock decline, Glassdoor sentiment, stalled growth)

## PHASE 5: Acquisition Target Analysis

Rank every company in the competitive landscape (including Metadata.io) as an acquisition target. For each, assess:
- Price (likely acquisition cost including any premium)
- Revenue multiple an acquirer would pay
- Strategic assets (what's defensible and hard to replicate?)
- Availability (is it actually for sale? motivated sellers?)
- Integration risk (how hard to absorb?)
- Growth trajectory (buying momentum or decline?)

Rank them worst-to-best acquisition target with detailed rationale for each. Identify who the most likely ACQUIRERS would be for the top targets (PE firms, strategic buyers, platform companies).

## PHASE 6: Who Would Benefit from Acquiring Metadata.io?

Specifically for Metadata.io:
- Tier 1: Perfect strategic fit + financial capacity + clear motive
- Tier 2: Strong fit with minor gaps
- Tier 3: Platform players who could leapfrog the market
- For each potential acquirer: what they'd pay, what they'd get, why it's smart for them, and likelihood rating
- Model a potential deal structure (price range, financing, synergies)

## PHASE 7: Strategic Position Summary

Synthesize everything into:
- The company's 3 biggest strengths
- The company's 3 biggest vulnerabilities
- The single most important strategic question the company faces right now
- What the company should do in the next 12 months to maximize its position (whether building for growth or building for exit)

---

## OUTPUT FORMAT

- Use tables wherever possible for comparisons
- Cite sources (publication names, @handles from X, subreddit names — not raw URLs)
- Clearly label estimates vs confirmed data
- Flag when information is behind paywalls or unavailable
- Be direct and opinionated — I want your actual assessment, not hedged "it depends" answers
- When you don't have data, say so and explain what the gap means for the analysis

## IMPORTANT

- Do NOT skip the ownership and capital structure analysis — this is often the most important part
- Do NOT treat all competitors as equal — weight them by relevance and market position
- Do research before forming opinions — read the actual press releases, financial disclosures, and user sentiment
- Cross-reference data points across multiple sources — if revenue figures conflict, note both and explain which is more credible
