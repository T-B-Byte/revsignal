When we say "Data-as-a-Service" we mean
Licensed access to pharosIQ's first-party contact universe and intelligent buying signals, delivered to the client's own stack via API, file, MCP, or self-service platform. The client owns and operates the data inside their systems. We refresh it.
Not leads. Not campaigns. Not content syndication. Not media spend. If it's a campaign with a CPL, it's not DaaS.
First, Second, Third, and Bid Stream Data: Know the Difference

The most common confusion in a data conversation is which kind of data the buyer is actually talking about. These four definitions are the table stakes of holding a credible conversation. Memorize them.

Type 01
First-Party Data
"You own it. You collected it."
Data the company collects directly from its own properties: owned websites, content, apps, CRM, events. The collector controls the source, the consent, and the chain of custody. Highest trust because nothing about it is borrowed or modeled.
Trust: Highest
Type 02
Second-Party Data
"Their first-party, shared with you."
Someone else's first-party data, shared with you directly under an agreement. Co-marketing partnerships, content syndication relationships, data co-ops between trusted partners. Quality depends on the partner's own data hygiene.
Trust: High (partner-dependent)
Type 03
Third-Party Data
"Aggregated and resold."
Data assembled by a vendor from many sources and sold to anyone who pays. Often cookie-based, scraped, or modeled. The buyer rarely knows where any specific record came from or how it was collected. Provenance is the weak point.
Trust: Mixed
Type 04
Bid Stream Data
"Captured from ad auctions."
A subset of third-party data. Collected from real-time bid requests in the programmatic ad ecosystem. Every ad slot auctioned leaks signals (URL, IP, device, geo). Aggregators package those bids and resell them as intent. Loud, noisy, and the chain of custody is weak.
Trust: Weak

Where pharosIQ sits

pharosIQ is first-party. Our intelligent buying signals come from real engagement on content we own and operate. Not aggregated. Not modeled. Not bid-stream. That is the structural advantage. When a prospect compares us to Bombora (co-op) or 6sense (modeled, partial bid-stream), this is the line that wins.

When a prospect says "data," your first job is figuring out which of the words above they mean. Most of the time they mean two or three at once. Section 2 (discovery questions) and Section 5 (triggers) help you decode it fast.

The 30-second version. pharosIQ has a data product line (DaaS) that runs alongside demand gen. When a client asks for contacts, intelligent buying signals, enrichment, an API feed, or a data partnership, that is a Tina conversation. Use the discovery questions in Section 2 to open the door, then listen for the trigger phrases in Section 5 once they're talking. Use the email template in Section 7 to hand off cleanly. Tina handles assessment, pricing, and the client conversation from there.

Section 2

Door-Opening Discovery Questions

Triggers tell you when a client has already revealed an opportunity. Discovery questions help you surface one before they say it out loud. Drop these into normal sales conversations to find the data layer hiding inside the account.

Open the door before they close it
Most DaaS opportunities are sitting one question away from the surface. Reps who ask these on every meaningful conversation surface DaaS deals their demand gen counterparts never see. Use one or two per call. You don't need to run the whole list.
Cluster 01
Their Stack & Sources
★ Lead with thisAre you using any second or third party data sources right now?
Where do your account lists come from, and what gets done to them before sellers see them?
What does your intent stack look like? Bombora, 6sense, ZoomInfo, Demandbase, anything else?
Is your data first-party, third-party, or a mix? How are you thinking about that mix going forward?
Cluster 02
Where the Cracks Are
★ Lead with thisWhen an account is interesting but you don't have the right contacts, what does your team do?
How fresh is the contact data your sellers are working with day-to-day?
Are reps ever hitting the wrong personas, or struggling to map the buying committee?
What's your match rate when you push an account list through enrichment?
Cluster 03
Build, Buy & Future State
★ Lead with thisIs your team building anything in-house with data? Internal scoring, AI products, custom workflows?
If you had a continuous feed of contact-level intent on your top accounts, where would it plug in?
What does your data partnership stack look like? Any vendors you're evaluating or unhappy with?
★ Always ask thisWho owns data partnerships and vendor decisions on your side?

How to use these in a real call

Pick one question from each cluster ahead of a call. Drop them in conversationally, not in sequence. The answers tell you whether a DaaS opportunity exists and where (stack gap, pain point, or strategic build). When you hear something interesting, jot the exact phrasing and use it in your handoff email to Tina.

Section 4

Who Tina Is Selling To, and the Use Cases They Buy For

Two views: the companies Tina is actively going after, and the use cases your existing demand gen customers (AWS, Cisco, IBM, and similar) typically have when there's a DaaS opportunity hiding inside the account.

Tina's Target Buyers (Three Buckets)

1. Data + Intent Platforms

Companies whose product is data. They license pharosIQ to enrich their own offering, fill coverage gaps, or layer signals on top.

Examples: Bombora, 6sense, ZoomInfo, Demandbase, TechTarget, Madison Logic. Often OEM or reseller structures.

2. Enterprise Tech + Brands

Large companies licensing data to power internal ABM, enrich CRM, or build a contact and intent layer their teams operate against directly.

Examples: AWS, Cisco, IBM, SAP, Lenovo, DocuSign. Same logos your demand gen team already sells into, often a different team and budget.

3. Agencies + Resellers

Demand gen, ABM, and data agencies that need a data layer to deliver services to their own clients. Reseller program territory.

Examples: white-label data feeds, embedded intent for agency tooling, packaged dossiers.

The Use Cases They Buy For
  • TAL-matched intent feeds. "Here's our target account list. Send us signals against it on a recurring cadence."
  • Contact enrichment and append. "Fill in the missing fields and personas on these accounts."
  • Persona expansion. Widen the outbound persona universe with Jobson title taxonomy.
  • Account discovery. "Who is surging on this topic right now, even if they're not in our list yet?"
  • Internal data layer. RevOps and Marketing Ops want pharosIQ data flowing into their warehouse, CDP, or SFDC.
  • White-label or OEM. Partner embeds pharosIQ data into their own product or service.
  • One-off intelligence. Account Architect, ABM Strategist, Deal DNA reports for a specific deal or initiative.
What Your Existing Customers (AWS, Cisco, IBM type) Typically Need

The same companies your demand gen team already sells leads to almost always have a separate team with a separate budget that buys data, not leads. Different buyer, different fiscal cycle. Don't assume "we already sell into them" means there's no DaaS opportunity. It usually means the opposite.

Listen for these patterns inside enterprise tech accounts:

  • ABM teams already buying account-level coverage from 6sense or Bombora and asking for contact-level depth and reasons to call.
  • RevOps running a Salesforce or HubSpot refresh before a major campaign, region launch, or fiscal year kickoff.
  • Regional field marketing in LATAM, APAC, or EMEA needing local contact coverage and persona depth their global data vendor doesn't deliver well.
  • Partner / channel teams wanting to score and prioritize the partner ecosystem, or surface co-sell triggers.
  • Product marketing launching a new SKU and building a fresh TAL who's in market for it.
  • Competitive intel teams tracking refresh cycles, stack migrations, and category shifts.
  • Data science / AI teams wanting raw data feeds for internal models, scoring, or AI products.

The lateral move

If you sell demand gen into a name like AWS, Cisco, or IBM, ask your contact: "Is there an ABM, RevOps, or data team here who licenses data directly?" Then ask for the intro. That's the lateral move that opens DaaS inside an existing logo.

Section 5

Keyword Triggers: When to Send the Conversation to Tina

Real phrases from active pipeline. If a client says something close to any of these, it's a DaaS conversation. Loop in Tina.

1
"We get account lists from 6sense / Bombora / Demandbase / Intentsify but we need contacts and business need signals before they reach our commercial team."
They have account-level coverage. They want contact-level depth and reasons to call. That's TAL-Matched Signal Delivery or Tier 2/3 Full Database Access.
2
"We need to enrich or append information to raw accounts."
Classic enrichment use case. They have a list, it's incomplete or thin, they want fields filled in. DaaS via API or file delivery.
3
"Our contact lists are stale or we keep hitting the wrong people."
Contact decay or persona mismatch. Jobson title expansion plus contact refresh against the pharosIQ universe.
4
"We need intent data on this account or this list."
Direct ask for intelligent buying signals. Could be a one-time match or a recurring feed.
5
"Can we get signals on who is in market for this category?"
Topic-driven discovery. They want surge data against a specific topic or category. AtlasIQ Surge Trending or Surge Radar territory.
6
"We're evaluating data vendors or building a data partnership."
Strategic. They are looking at vendor consolidation, OEM, or reseller relationships. Always loop Tina in early.
7
"We need something that plugs into our tech stack or data layer."
Integration ask. CRM, CDP, data warehouse, marketing automation. API or file delivery DaaS.
8
"We're looking at enrichment or an API data feed."
Specific delivery ask. They've already decided what they want, they're shopping for the source.

When in doubt, ask one question

"Are you looking for a campaign that delivers leads, or are you looking to license data your team uses inside your own systems?" If the answer is anything but "campaign with leads," it's a Tina conversation.

Two emails from active pipeline that show what good looks like.

Example 1: Spotting the trigger in the wild Trigger Spot

Carolina was on the ground in Brazil, heard a Cohesity stakeholder describe an account-list-plus-enrichment need, and flagged it the same day. Short email, but it nailed the trigger phrase verbatim.

From: Carolina Williams <cwilliams@pharosiq.com>
To: Tina Bean <tbean@pharosiq.com>
Cc: Gustavo Nunez
Subject: Data Enrichment Opportunity: Cohesity LATAM
Date: Monday, April 20, 2026
Hi there Tina!! How are you? I was in Brazil last week and came across an opportunity we believe you may be able to support. Our Cohesity stakeholder for LATAM expressed a need to "enhance/enrich" their Accounts List they receive from 6sense with contacts/intel/business needs "feeds" to enrich their CRM before reaching their commercial team, or append information to these "raw" accounts. Will you be available this week so that we can discuss this further? We could also introduce you to the client as well to see if there is anything we can offer them? Thank you, Carolina

Why this worked: Carolina captured the client's exact language ("enhance/enrich," "before reaching their commercial team," "raw accounts"). Tina knew within 30 seconds this was Trigger 1 and Trigger 2 combined. Even with no other context, the trigger phrasing was enough to act on.

Example 2: A clean handoff with everything in one email Gold Standard

Valentina took it one step further. After Carolina flagged the trigger, Valentina sent a structured handoff with every field Tina needs to assess fit. This is the format to copy.

Hi Tina! Nice to meet you through here. I am Valentina from the LATAM sales team. I am reaching out because I have a client potentially interested in acquiring signals (and maybe CS).
