DaaS Product Framework: Tiered Licensing Model

pharosIQ Confidential March 2026
The core constraint: Contacts + content consumption data is directly competitive with pharosIQ's lead generation business. A buyer with both can self-serve the exact product Marty's team sells. Contacts + intent topic signals (without content detail) is not competitive. It tells you who is researching what, but not which specific content they consumed. This distinction is the foundation of the tiered model below. Tiers are additive: each includes everything in the tier below it. Tier 1 (accounts) and Tier 2 (personas) are open to all buyers. Contact-level data (Tiers 3 and 4) carries buyer restrictions. Each tier defines what we sell, who can buy it, and who is excluded, so there is no ambiguity in the field.
Scope: These tiers represent access to the full universe of pharosIQ data (360M+ contacts, 1.6M engaged signals, 650+ intent categories). Buyers who only need coverage for their own target account list receive a targeted account feed, a scoped delivery of the same tiered data, filtered to the buyer's named accounts. Targeted feeds follow the same tier structure and buyer restrictions. Pricing adjusts based on volume and scope.

What Makes Surge Dossiers Possible: Data Provenance
A Surge Dossier is not a generic company report. It is an AI-synthesized intelligence document assembled from pharosIQ's proprietary first-party signal chain. Every data point in the dossier traces back to a known content engagement on a pharosIQ-owned property. That provenance is what makes the dossier defensible, accurate, and impossible for competitors to replicate with scraped or co-op data.
What goes into a dossier: Which personas at the target company are actively researching which topics, how that activity has changed over time, what seniority levels and job functions are involved, which intent categories are surging vs. cooling, and what that pattern means for the buyer's sales approach. The dossier connects the signal to the strategy.
Why competitors can't build this: Intentsify, Bombora, and other co-op/bid-stream providers can tell you an account is surging. They cannot tell you which specific personas are driving the surge, because they don't have first-party content engagement at the contact level. pharosIQ can, because the signal originates from content the company owns (CONTENTgine + MRP). That data provenance is the moat.

Land and Expand

Every deal starts with a signal feed or a snapshot package. That's the land. The expand is the upsell from Snapshot to Monitor, from Monitor 25 to Monitor 100, and from signals-only to signals + dossiers. The signal feed creates dependency (their outbound runs on it). The dossiers create stickiness (their reps rely on it for prep). The monitor creates an annual renewal that grows with the buyer's account coverage. This is not a one-time product sale. It's a recurring revenue engine.
